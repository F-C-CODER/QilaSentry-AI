NAHR-I-BIHISHT – STREAM OF PARADISE

You are standing near the Nahr-i-Bihisht, which translates to the "Stream of Paradise." This elegant water channel was one of the most remarkable features of the Red Fort and formed the heart of the emperor's private palace complex. During the reign of Shah Jahan, water from the Yamuna River was lifted through an advanced hydraulic system at the Shahi Burj and directed into this channel, allowing it to flow through the royal apartments, gardens, and pavilions.

The Nahr-i-Bihisht was much more than a source of water. It was designed to symbolize the rivers of paradise described in Islamic tradition and played a central role in creating a peaceful and luxurious environment for the Mughal court. As the cool water flowed through marble channels, it helped regulate temperature, enhanced the beauty of the palaces, and created a soothing atmosphere with the gentle sound of running water.

Many of the most important structures of the Red Fort, including the Khas Mahal, Rang Mahal, and Diwan-i-Khas, were connected by this stream. At its peak, the flowing water reflected sunlight, marble surfaces, and intricate decorations, creating an experience that impressed visitors from around the world.

Even today, the remains of the Nahr-i-Bihisht offer a glimpse into the engineering brilliance, artistic vision, and refined lifestyle of the Mughal Empire.

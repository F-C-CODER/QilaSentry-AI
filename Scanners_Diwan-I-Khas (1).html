DIWAN-I-KHAS – HALL OF PRIVATE AUDIENCE

You are now at the Diwan-i-Khas, the Hall of Private Audience, one of the most luxurious and exclusive spaces within the Red Fort. Constructed in 1648, this magnificent marble pavilion was reserved for meetings between the Mughal emperor, royal family members, high-ranking nobles, foreign ambassadors, and distinguished guests.

Unlike the public atmosphere of the Diwan-i-Aam, only a select few were permitted to enter this hall. The structure was once adorned with gold, silver, precious stones, and intricate floral inlay work.

The famous Peacock Throne, regarded as one of the most extravagant thrones ever created, originally stood here. Through the center of the hall flowed the Nahr-i-Bihisht, or "Stream of Paradise," enhancing its beauty and cooling the surroundings.

Above its arches is the famous inscription: "If there is a paradise on earth, it is this, it is this, it is this."

Standing here today offers a glimpse into the immense wealth, refinement, and artistic brilliance of the Mughal court at its peak.

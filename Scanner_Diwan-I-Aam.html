DIWAN-I-AAM – HALL OF PUBLIC AUDIENCE

You are standing in the Diwan-i-Aam, or Hall of Public Audience, one of the most important spaces within the Red Fort. Built by Emperor Shah Jahan in the mid-17th century, this grand hall was where the Mughal emperor met common citizens, heard petitions, settled disputes, and addressed matters of governance.

The hall was designed with elegant red sandstone columns and a series of arches that created an impressive yet accessible setting for public interaction. At the far end stood the emperor's marble throne canopy, where Shah Jahan and his successors would sit while listening to the concerns of their subjects.

The Diwan-i-Aam symbolized the emperor's role as a just ruler and served as the administrative heart of the Mughal Empire.

Imagine hundreds of citizens, nobles, soldiers, and officials gathered here, waiting for an audience with one of the most powerful rulers in the world.
